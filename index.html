About

Observers → Researchers

We love walking! So busted sidewalks aren't kosher. When we noticed how frequently our routes turned messy, precarious, or otherwise unwalkable- we knew something was up. We read (somewhere- enter source) pedestrians made up about 30% of Austin roadway fatalities in 2015.(!!!) Okay, so we weren't overreacting. We got more curious.

Researchers → Collectors

The issue has been raised before. Some data: The 2009 Austin Sidewalk Master Plan calculated the price tag of needed sidewalk construction and improvements at $944 million. Yet the municipal Neigh­bor­hood Connectivity Division, that falls under the Public Works Department, has an annual budget of just $5 million to $10 million. This helps explain the state of our sidewalks (sigh).

Collectors → Advocates

On the bright side, people have solved this probem before. It turns out the benefits of accessible sidewalks are HUGE, they're at the heart of Walkable City: How Downtown Can Save America One Step at a Time. Author Jeff Speck discusses how walkability promotes improved health, lowers transportation costs per household, and frees time for residents to enjoy themselves.
